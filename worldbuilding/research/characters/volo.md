# Volothamp Geddarm: The Famous Chronicler

## Overview

Volothamp Geddarm, universally known as "Volo," is the most famous—and perhaps most notorious—chronicler and travel writer in all Faerûn. His name graces numerous guidebooks, bestiaries, and travel accounts, making him a household name across the Sword Coast and beyond. His works blend genuine scholarship with self-aggrandizement, factual reporting with wild exaggeration, and useful information with dangerous inaccuracies.

Volo is as much a character in his own right as any adventurer. His flamboyant personality, his penchant for trouble, and his willingness to hire others to extract him from his messes have made him a fixture in taverns and adventuring circles alike.

## Key Information

### Profession

- **Explorer and Raconteur** - Travels widely and tells tales of his journeys
- **Author** - Has written numerous guidebooks and reference works
- **Quest-Giver** - Known for hiring adventurers to solve his problems

### Known Works

**Volo's Guide to Monsters** - His most recently published work as of the Waterdeep: Dragon Heist timeline. This bestiary has become a standard reference for adventurers seeking information about dangerous creatures.

**Volo's Waterdeep Enchiridion** - A comprehensive guide to the City of Splendors, covering:
- Entering Waterdeep
- A Long History (in Brief)
- Surviving in the City
- The Wards of Waterdeep
- The City's Splendors

The term "enchiridion" refers to a handbook or manual—Volo's guide to Waterdeep is literally "Volo's Waterdeep Handbook."

### Personality

**The Self-Promoter:**
Volo never misses an opportunity to promote his latest work or mention his past accomplishments. His introduction to adventurers typically includes references to his recently completed book tour.

**The Trouble-Magnet:**
Volo has a remarkable talent for getting himself and his friends into dangerous situations. His personal relationships sometimes put innocent people at risk.

**The Generous (If Unconventional) Patron:**
When Volo hires adventurers to rescue his kidnapped friend Floon Blagmaar, he rewards them not with gold but with *the deed to a property in Waterdeep*—a tavern called Trollskull Manor. This is both generous and characteristic: Volo gives lavishly when he can, but often in unexpected ways because he lacks liquid funds.

### The Yawning Portal Connection

Volo is a well-known figure at the Yawning Portal inn and tavern in Waterdeep:

> "Welcome to Waterdeep, the Crown of the North, where a wondrous tale of urban adventure is about to unfold. Our story begins with the gathering of adventurers at the Yawning Portal Inn and Tavern. Volothamp Geddarm, the famous explorer and raconteur, has a quest for them—one that entangles the characters in a bitter conflict between two nefarious organizations." — Waterdeep: Dragon Heist, Introduction

The Yawning Portal serves as a natural gathering place for adventurers, making it an ideal location for Volo to recruit help when he needs it.

### Quest: The Missing Friend

In Waterdeep: Dragon Heist, Volo approaches adventurers with a straightforward rescue mission:

**The Problem:** His friend Floon Blagmaar has disappeared, apparently kidnapped.

**The Twist:** Floon was caught up in a case of mistaken identity. The kidnappers actually wanted Renaer Neverember, the estranged son of the former Open Lord.

**The Reward:** Trollskull Manor, a property in the North Ward that includes:
- A former tavern (closed for years)
- Residence on upper floors
- A poltergeist haunting that must be dealt with

This quest exemplifies Volo's role in the Realms: he connects adventurers with opportunities, provides them with resources (however unconventional), and propels them into larger storylines.

### Relationship with Elminster

The two most famous chroniclers of the Realms have a... complicated relationship. Elminster is known to complain about Volo's work:

> "You should hear Elminster when he goes on about Volo!" — Ed Greenwood, Elminster's Forgotten Realms

The nature of these complaints isn't specified, but one can imagine the ancient sage's frustration with Volo's tendency toward exaggeration, inaccuracy, and self-promotion. Where Elminster aims for comprehensive accuracy (even while withholding dangerous secrets), Volo aims for entertainment value and commercial success.

## Writing Style and Reputation

### The Volo Voice

Volo's writing is characterized by:
- First-person narrative with personal anecdotes
- Confident assertions (sometimes despite insufficient evidence)
- Self-referential humor
- Practical advice mixed with entertainment
- Occasional dangerous inaccuracies

### Scholarly Reception

Volo's works are viewed with a mixture of appreciation and skepticism by serious scholars. His guidebooks contain genuinely useful information, but must be read with critical attention. His tendency to embellish, speculate, or simply make things up when he lacks reliable sources means that treating his works as authoritative can be hazardous.

That said, Volo's books *sell*. He has a talent for making dry subjects engaging and for packaging information in accessible formats. His works reach audiences that would never read a more rigorous scholarly treatment.

## Character Role in Adventures

Volo serves several functions in D&D adventures:

1. **Quest Hook** - He provides missions and rewards
2. **Information Source** - His guidebooks offer (imperfect) intelligence
3. **Comic Relief** - His personality and troubles provide humor
4. **Property Granter** - He gives adventurers a base of operations
5. **Faction Connection** - He connects to broader storylines

## Contradictions & Notes

### The Accuracy Question

Volo's works are simultaneously useful and unreliable. This creates an interesting dynamic where adventurers must decide how much to trust his information—and may suffer consequences when they trust too much or too little.

### Financial Situation

Despite his fame, Volo often seems to lack ready coin. He rewards the adventurers who rescue Floon with property rather than gold, and his friend Floon is described as a "handsome simpleton" rather than a wealthy patron. This suggests Volo's wealth is primarily in reputation and property rather than liquid assets.

### Pronunciation

The official pronunciation is provided:
- Volothamp: VOH-loh-thamp
- Geddarm: geh-DARM
